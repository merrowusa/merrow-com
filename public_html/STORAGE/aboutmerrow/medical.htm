Medical Applications

Pulmonary Surgery
Working with a leading provider of synthetic human tissue and membrane, and using sterile suture material, Merrow specially adapted a releasable chain link stitch used to afix staple-line-reinforcement material to a surgical stapler. This highly specialized application enables surgeons to treat emphyzema and lung cancer patients who have lost sections of their lungs with a less invasive procedure and reduces post-operative pulmonary air leaks.

Regenerative Medicine
Merrow Machine partnered with a pioneering company in the field of regenerative medicine to precisely sew together small sections of human derma to form larger sections of sterile skin material used for burn treatment, skin grafts, plastic and reconstructive surgery, periodontal surgery, green-stick fractures and other procedures. Because Merrow machines use all-metal components, the entire machine can be placed in an autoclave for sterilization. Merrow used special feeding mechanisms and machines with nickel-plated components, to produce a miniaturized flat butt-seam stitch that meets this application’s demanding requirements for precision tissue joining and sterile production. This advanced process means that acellular tissue can now be produced in a more controlled environment, which ultimately reduces the risk of infection, promotes healing and results in better outcomes for patients.
