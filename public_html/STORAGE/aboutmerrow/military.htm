Military and Aerospace Applications

Since before the first World War, Merrow machines have fulfilled government contracts to sew blankets and socks for our servicemen and women. In fact, Merrow developed the federal stitch standard for hosiery, which is part of the military specification for socks used by our troops.

Today, the armed forces of the United States rely on Merrow Machine’s expertise when it comes to joining and seaming advanced materials. The company sews tent covers for tanks that protect them from the elements while reducing the infrared signature of their engines. Merrow also specializes in seaming space-age materials like woven carbon fiber and Kevlar used in military aviation for their high strength to weight ratio and ballistic resistance. You’ll find Merrow stitching at work on the Space Shuttle and a variety of missile classes, where a butt seam stitch is used to sew together bronze screen, which is then laminated with high-tech materials to fabricate the rocket engines’ directional cones.

In addition, Merrow sews a special seam for neoprene survival/immersion suits used to protect sailors, aircrews and rescuers from hypothermia and exposure in extreme ocean and cold water environment conditions. The stitch joins and reinforces sections of neoprene without compromising the material’s vital insulating characteristics with full-depth needle-hole-penetration during processing. When it comes to mission-critical applications, Merrow has the knowledge and experience to deliver a reliable solution.
