oseph M. Merrow and son founded the first knitting mill in America. Here, the Merrow company developed knitting machines which automated the process for seaming and edge finishing a variety of knit garments including blouses, hosiery, underwear, sweaters and gloves.

   



 
Other Merrow contributions followed: the first crochet machine, the first shell stitching machine and the first butt-seaming machine for continuous processing of textile fabrics. The overedge stitch the company pioneered is known throughout the commercial sewing industry as "Merrowing".

A principal producer and retailer of women's intimate apparel and swimwear sought Merrow's assistance in the production of a shawl collar on a popular bathrobe style. The retailer wanted a quality, uniform, stitch that could be stitched on a single pass through the sewing machine. Merrow adapted an existing machine with a special mechanism to double productivity, reduce cost, and improve the quality of the finished garment.


Leading farm and heavy equipment manufacturers rely on a well-known tire and rubber company for sturdy drive and fan belts. The producer turned to Merrow to develop a solution essential to its manufacturing process. The assignment: to develop a high-strength, no-overlap stitch for the rubber belts' cloth backing. Merrow developed a proprietary butted seam stitch which secured the fabric on a cylindrical mandrel during a high-stress manufacturing process.
